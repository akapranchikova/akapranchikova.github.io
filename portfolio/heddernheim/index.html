Adhering strictly to modernist
principles, the first conceptual designs
of New Frankfurt prioritized efficient
use of space, incorporating green
areas, communal facilities,
and standardized building techniques
to maximize both livability
and aesthetic harmony.
My first design conception powerfully
illustrates the principles of using
space wisely through meticulously

In the 20th century, the district
of Heddernheim became a focal point
for the innovative New Frankfurt
project, a hallmark of modernist urban planning and architecture.
Spearheaded by architect Ernst May, the initiative sought to address
the post-World War I housing crisis
by creating functional, affordable
living spaces.
